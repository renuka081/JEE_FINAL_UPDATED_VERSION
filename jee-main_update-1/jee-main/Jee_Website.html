JEE MAINS AND ADVANCE DETAILS

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is an engineering entrance assessment conducted for admission to various engineering colleges in India. This highly competitive exam is essential for students aspiring to pursue undergraduate engineering programs at prestigious institutions like the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), and Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs).

Importance of JEE

Cracking JEE opens doors to premier engineering institutions providing top-notch education and career opportunities. It tests a student's knowledge, analytical abilities, time management skills, and perseverance.

JEE Main and Advanced Selection Process

IIT JEE Exam is for the students who have a 10+2 certificate from a recognized board/school.

This examination is held in parts every year. To pass this evaluation, the students will first have to clear the Mains examination following which they will qualify for the JEE Advanced exam.

JEE Mains and Advanced Exam Recruitment

Candidates must first understand the IIT JEE Advanced / Mains selection process to get an idea of the selection process and prepare themselves for the evaluation process.

The IIT JEE Mains and Advanced exam recruitment are done in two parts: an IIT JEE Mains and an Advanced exam.

JEE Mains

Eligibility Criteria: Candidates must have completed their 10+2 or equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. They can attempt JEE Mains a maximum of three times in consecutive years.

Exam Pattern: JEE Mains comprises two papers: Paper 1 for B.E./B.Tech and Paper 2 for B.Arch/B.Planning. It is a computer-based test with 90 questions, carrying 300 marks in total.

Syllabus: The syllabus includes topics from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, following the NCERT curriculum.

Subject Number of Questions Total Marks
Physics 30 100
Chemistry 30 100
Mathematics 30 100

JEE Advanced

Eligibility Criteria: Only the top 2.5 lakh candidates (approximately) who qualify JEE Mains are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced. Additionally, candidates must meet other eligibility criteria, including age limit and number of attempts.

Exam Pattern: JEE Advanced consists of two compulsory papers (Paper 1 and Paper 2) conducted on the same day. Both papers are of three hours duration and cover questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

Syllabus: The syllabus for JEE Advanced is more extensive and in-depth compared to JEE Mains. It includes advanced topics from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics that require thorough preparation and conceptual understanding.

Having the right study material is crucial for JEE preparation. Here are some recommended resources:

JEE Mains and Advanced Syllabus

JEE Mains examination is an online exam conducted in two stages: JEE Mains and JEE Advanced and then the final selection process. For clearing the exam, it is necessary to know every topic or subject that will be asked in the examination. Here we are going to provide the updated syllabus of JEE Mains 2021 and Advanced 2021.

JEE Mains Examination consist of these three main subjects:

  • JEE Mains Physics Syllabus
  • JEE Mains Mathematics Syllabus
  • JEE Mains Chemistry Syllabus

JEE Mains April 2021 Paper 1 Syllabus

JEE Physics Syllabus:

Physics and measurement, electrostatics, current electricity, oscillation and waves, thermodynamics, rotational movements, laws of motion, work, energy, and power, kinetic theory of gases, electromagnetic induction, and alternating currents, optics, kinematics, gravitation, properties of solid, liquid, and gas.

JEE Mains Chemistry Syllabus:

These three types of chemistry is described:

Organic chemistry:

Purification and characterization of organic compounds, organic compounds containing halogens, chemistry in everyday life, and some basic principles of organic chemistry.

Physical chemistry:

Basic concepts in chemistry, atomic structure, equilibrium, chemical thermodynamics, chemical bonding and molecular structure, states of matter, chemical kinetics.

Inorganic chemistry:

Classification of elements and periodicity in properties, hydrogen D-and f-block elements, environmental chemistry, block elements, block elements group 13 to group 18 elements.

Mathematics:

Sets, functions and relations, internal calculus, matrices and determinants, complex numbers and quadratic equations, mathematical induction, binomial theorem, statistics and probability, vector algebra, permutations and combinations, mathematical reasoning.

JEE Mains April 2021 Paper-II Syllabus:

  • Aptitude
  • Mathematics
  • Drawing Test

Paper-III

  • Mathematics
  • Aptitude Test
  • Planning Based Questions

JEE Advanced consists of two parts:

Which consists of JEE Advanced 2021 Paper I and II syllabus:

Paper-I

  • Chemistry

Paper-II

Physics:

Thermodynamics, optics, modern physics, magnetism, mechanics, and electricity

Chemistry:

It is divided into three patterns:

Physical chemistry:

Gaseous and liquid state, atomic structure and chemical bonding, energetics, chemical equilibrium, electrochemistry, solutions, surface chemistry, nuclear chemistry, solid-state

Inorganic Chemistry:

Isolation, preparation of and properties of non-metals, compounds and transition elements, ores and minerals, extractive metallurgy

Organic chemistry:

Preparation, properties of alkalis, and alkynes, benzene, phenols, the reaction of carbohydrates, amino acids, and uses of essential polymers.

Mathematics:

Algebra, matrices, trigonometry, probability, analytical geometry, differential, and integral calculus, vectors.
